BACHELOR OF SCIENCE IN COMPUTER SCIENCE (BSCS)
Program Requirements 2024-2025
=============================================================

PROGRAM OVERVIEW
The Bachelor of Science in Computer Science requires completion of 120 credit hours, including general education requirements, CS core courses, mathematics requirements, and elective credits. Students must maintain a minimum GPA of 2.0 in CS coursework to remain in good standing.

---

RESIDENCY REQUIREMENT
At least 30 of the final 45 credit hours toward the degree must be completed at State University (transfer credits do not count toward this requirement). Additionally, at least 18 credit hours in CS courses numbered 300 or above must be earned at State University.

---

GENERAL EDUCATION REQUIREMENTS (30 credits)
All students must complete General Education requirements as specified in the University Catalog. These include:
- English Composition (6 credits): ENG 101 and ENG 102
- Quantitative Reasoning (satisfied by MATH 115 or higher)
- Social Sciences (6 credits)
- Humanities (6 credits)
- Natural Sciences with Lab (8 credits)
- Diversity and Ethics (3 credits)

---

MATHEMATICS REQUIREMENTS (16 credits)
Required:
- MATH 115 - Calculus I (4 credits)
- MATH 116 - Calculus II (4 credits) [Required for AI/ML Track; Optional for other tracks - see Track Requirements below]
- MATH 210 - Discrete Mathematics (3 credits) [or CS 220 - equivalent]
- STAT 201 - Probability and Statistics (3 credits) [or MATH 310 - equivalent]

Note: MATH 116 is required only for the AI/ML concentration. Students in the General CS track may substitute MATH 116 with a 3-credit CS elective.

---

CS CORE REQUIREMENTS (42 credits)
All CS majors must complete the following core courses. A grade of C or better is required in each core course.

Foundation Courses (15 credits):
- CS 101 - Introduction to Programming (3 credits)
- CS 102 - Data Structures and Algorithms I (3 credits)
- CS 201 - Data Structures and Algorithms II (3 credits)
- CS 210 - Computer Organization and Architecture (3 credits)
- CS 220 - Discrete Mathematics for CS (3 credits) [or MATH 210 - equivalent; only one may count]

Systems Courses (9 credits):
- CS 302 - Operating Systems (3 credits)
- CS 310 - Software Engineering (3 credits)
- CS 330 - Computer Networks (3 credits)

Theory and Languages (6 credits):
- CS 320 - Theory of Computation (3 credits)
- CS 350 - Programming Languages (3 credits)

Databases (3 credits):
- CS 301 - Database Systems (3 credits)

Capstone (6 credits) [Senior Year Only]:
- CS 450 - Senior Capstone Project I (3 credits)
- CS 451 - Senior Capstone Project II (3 credits)

---

CONCENTRATION / TRACK REQUIREMENTS (Choose One - 12 credits)

Track 1: Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning
Required courses:
- CS 340 - Artificial Intelligence (3 credits)
- CS 401 - Machine Learning (3 credits)
- CS 461 - Natural Language Processing (3 credits)
Additional required math for this track:
- MATH 116 - Calculus II (4 credits)
- MATH 301 - Linear Algebra Advanced (3 credits)
Elective (choose 1, 3 credits): CS 470 (Computer Graphics), CS 420 (Distributed Systems), CS 480 (Special Topics)

Track 2: Systems & Security
Required courses:
- CS 420 - Distributed Systems (3 credits)
- CS 430 - Computer Security (3 credits)
Elective (choose 2, 3 credits each): CS 410 (Compilers), CS 340 (AI), CS 480 (Special Topics)

Track 3: General Computer Science
Required courses:
- CS 340 - Artificial Intelligence (3 credits)
Electives (choose 3, 3 credits each from 300/400-level CS courses not otherwise required):
- CS 301, CS 410, CS 420, CS 430, CS 461, CS 470, CS 480, CS 490

---

ELECTIVE CREDITS
Students must complete sufficient elective credits (CS or non-CS) to reach 120 total credit hours. At least 9 of the CS elective credits must be at the 300-level or above.

---

MAXIMUM CREDIT LIMITS
- CS 490 (Independent Study): Maximum 6 credits may count toward graduation requirements.
- CS 480 (Special Topics): Maximum 6 credits (across multiple semesters) may count toward graduation requirements.
- Transfer credits: Maximum 60 transfer credits accepted toward the 120-credit degree.

---

ACADEMIC STANDING AND PROGRESSION REQUIREMENTS
1. Students must earn a grade of C or better in all CS core courses.
2. Students earning a D or F in a CS core course must retake the course before advancing to courses that list it as a prerequisite.
3. Students may repeat a CS course a maximum of 2 times (3 total attempts). A third attempt requires written petition to the CS Academic Appeals Committee.
4. Students must achieve Senior standing (90+ credit hours completed) before enrolling in CS 450.
5. Students with a CS GPA below 2.0 for two consecutive semesters will be placed on Academic Probation and may not enroll in upper-division CS courses (300+) without advisor approval.

---

GRADUATION REQUIREMENTS CHECKLIST
To graduate, students must:
□ Complete all General Education requirements (30 credits)
□ Complete all Mathematics requirements (16 credits)
□ Complete all CS Core courses (42 credits) with grades of C or better
□ Complete one Concentration Track (12 credits)
□ Complete sufficient electives to reach 120 total credits
□ Achieve a cumulative GPA of 2.0 or higher
□ Achieve a CS major GPA of 2.0 or higher
□ Satisfy the Residency requirement (30 of final 45 credits at State University)
□ Complete CS 450 and CS 451 (Capstone sequence)
□ Apply for graduation at least one semester before expected completion

---

DEGREE MAP (Typical 4-Year Plan, General Track)

Year 1, Fall: CS 101, MATH 115, ENG 101, General Ed (2 courses)
Year 1, Spring: CS 102, MATH 116, ENG 102, CS 210/MATH 210, General Ed

Year 2, Fall: CS 201, CS 210, STAT 201, General Ed (2 courses)
Year 2, Spring: CS 301, CS 302, CS 310, General Ed

Year 3, Fall: CS 302 (if not taken), CS 320, CS 340, CS 330, Elective
Year 3, Spring: CS 350, CS 330 (if not taken), CS 410 or Track elective, Elective

Year 4, Fall: CS 450, Track elective, Elective (2 courses)
Year 4, Spring: CS 451, Track elective, Elective (2 courses)

Note: This degree map is illustrative. Actual plans vary based on AP credits, transfer credits, course availability, and individual circumstances. Consult your academic advisor.
