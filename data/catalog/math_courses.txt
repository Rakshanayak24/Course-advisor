MATH 100 - Pre-Calculus
Credits: 3
Prerequisites: Placement exam score of 40 or higher OR instructor consent
Co-requisites: None
Description: Functions and their graphs, polynomial and rational functions, exponential and logarithmic functions, trigonometry. Intended for students who need additional preparation before Calculus.
Offered: Fall, Spring, Summer
Notes: Credit does not count toward CS or Engineering degree requirements.

MATH 115 - Calculus I
Credits: 4
Prerequisites: MATH 100 with a grade of C or better OR placement exam score of 70 or higher
Co-requisites: None
Description: Limits, continuity, derivatives (definition, rules, implicit differentiation, related rates), applications of differentiation (optimization, curve sketching, mean value theorem), introduction to integration (antiderivatives, definite integral, Fundamental Theorem of Calculus).
Offered: Fall, Spring, Summer
Notes: This is a core requirement for CS, Engineering, and Physics majors.

MATH 116 - Calculus II
Credits: 4
Prerequisites: MATH 115 with a grade of C or better
Co-requisites: None
Description: Techniques of integration (substitution, parts, partial fractions, trigonometric), improper integrals, sequences and series (convergence tests, Taylor/Maclaurin series), parametric equations and polar coordinates.
Offered: Fall, Spring
Notes: Required for CS majors who pursue the Applied Computing or AI/ML track.

MATH 210 - Discrete Mathematics
Credits: 3
Prerequisites: MATH 115 with a grade of C or better OR CS 101 with a grade of C or better
Co-requisites: None
Description: Propositional logic, predicate logic, proof techniques (direct, contradiction, induction), sets, functions, relations, counting, probability, graph theory basics, and trees.
Offered: Fall, Spring
Notes: Cross-listed as CS 220. Credit may be received for only one of MATH 210 or CS 220.

MATH 250 - Linear Algebra
Credits: 3
Prerequisites: MATH 115 with a grade of C or better
Co-requisites: None
Description: Vectors and vector spaces, matrix operations, systems of linear equations (Gaussian elimination), determinants, eigenvalues and eigenvectors, orthogonality, linear transformations.
Offered: Fall, Spring
Notes: Required for CS students pursuing AI/ML track. Also listed as MATH 301 in some older catalogs; MATH 250 and MATH 301 are equivalent.

MATH 301 - Linear Algebra (Advanced)
Credits: 3
Prerequisites: MATH 116 with a grade of C or better
Co-requisites: None
Description: Equivalent content to MATH 250 but with more rigorous proofs and deeper theoretical treatment. Abstract vector spaces, inner product spaces, spectral theorem, Jordan normal form.
Offered: Spring only
Notes: MATH 301 and MATH 250 are NOT interchangeable for all requirements. CS 401 (Machine Learning) requires MATH 301. Check with your advisor if you are unsure which to take.

MATH 310 - Probability and Statistics for Engineers
Credits: 3
Prerequisites: MATH 116 with a grade of C or better
Co-requisites: None
Description: Probability spaces, random variables, distributions (binomial, Poisson, normal, exponential), expectation and variance, joint distributions, central limit theorem, confidence intervals, hypothesis testing, linear regression.
Offered: Fall, Spring
Notes: Cross-listed as STAT 201. CS students may use either version to satisfy the Probability requirement.

STAT 201 - Probability and Statistics
Credits: 3
Prerequisites: MATH 115 with a grade of C or better
Co-requisites: None
Description: Descriptive statistics, probability fundamentals, discrete and continuous distributions, sampling distributions, estimation, hypothesis testing, chi-square tests, regression and correlation.
Offered: Fall, Spring
Notes: Cross-listed as MATH 310. Credit may be received for only one of STAT 201 or MATH 310.
